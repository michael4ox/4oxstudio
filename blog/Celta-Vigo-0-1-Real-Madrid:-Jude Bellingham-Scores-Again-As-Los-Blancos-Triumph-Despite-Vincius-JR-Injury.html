CELTA VIGO 0-1 REAL MADRID: JUDE BELLINGHAM SCORES AGAIN AS LOS BLANCOS TRIUMPH DESPITE VINICIUS JR INJURY

Celta Vigo proved dauntless opponents for Real Madrid on Friday night and Los Blancos once again had Jude Bellingham to thank as his goal sealed the three points. Real saw Vinicius Jr go off injured early in the first half in a blow that robbed them of one of their chief creative outlets, but Bellingham - in his advanced role under Carlo Ancelotti - got free to convert a late Toni Kroos corner.

Jude Bellingham scored again for Real Madrid as they earned a late 1-0 win at Celta Vigo in La Liga on Friday night. Los Blancos were looking to extend their winning streak after two consecutive victories, already top of La Liga after falling short of Barcelona last season.

Celta meanwhile were looking to add to their solitary point, and were unlucky to see Jorgen Strand Larsen have a goal disallowed as the referee believed new Madrid keeper Kepa Arrizabalaga had been impeded by the player seconds earlie

Carlo Ancelotti will have been disappointed to see Vinicius Jr. hobble off with an injury midway through the first half and Real were struggling for rhythm.
In the second half, Toni Kroos and Luka Modric were introduced just after the hour mark. Four minutes later, Rodrygo was brought down by Ivan Villar to earn a penalty, only for the keeper to save well from the Brazilian. But with just under 10 minutes remaining, Bellingham’s close-range header won it for the visitors.

TALKING POINT - WILL ANCELOTTI REALLY STAY OUT OF THE MARKET?

The Italian coach proclaimed once again that Real had done their business, but he must be tempted to rethink that promise.
The injury to VInicius, if serious, leaves Ancelotti with Joselu to play up front. He showed little against Celta to show that he can be a serious first-choice striker for the long term.
With a few days left, the prognosis for Vinicius could change the calculation.

PLAYER OF THE MATCH - JUDE BELLINGHAM (REAL MADRID)?

With Vinicius out, the evolution of Bellingham into a goalscoring midfielder could be a vital change for the Spaniards as they look to reclaim La Liga.
Four goals in three games for the former Borussia Dortmund man suggest that the huge outlay on the player is going to prove a bargain rather than a waste.
